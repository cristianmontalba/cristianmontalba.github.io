<div style="text-align: justify;">

<p>I am an MRI researcher and imaging specialist driven by the use of computational methods to deliver effective solutions for real-world neurological challenges in <strong>clinical translation</strong>. With a medical imaging and radiographer background, my research interest lies at the intersection of <strong>quantitative neuroimaging, interpretable machine learning, and open science</strong>, which I have explored through software development and computational neuroimaging analysis. I believe that technical expertise, along with a deep understanding of clinical workflows, is fundamental to driving meaningful progress in complex medical tasks.</p>

<p>I have led and engineered neuroimaging pipelines across topics as diverse as mapping microstructural disruption in <strong>Multiple Sclerosis</strong>, extracting radiomic features for <strong>glioma characterization</strong>, and analyzing the nigrostriatal pathway in <strong>Psychosis</strong>. One fundamental challenge across these projects is the "black box" nature of AI models and data variability, which I have addressed through the incorporation of <strong>domain-specific neuroanatomical constraints</strong> and <strong>interpretable machine learning frameworks</strong> to ensure predictive models provide reliable insights that clinicians can trust.</p>

<p>Beyond my core research, I am deeply committed to open science and global education. As a <strong>co-leader of international initiatives under the ESMRMB</strong>, I actively work to democratize MRI expertise and technical access across low- and middle-income countries (LMICs).</p>

</div>
