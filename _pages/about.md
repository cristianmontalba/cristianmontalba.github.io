I am an **MRI Researcher and Medical Imaging Technologist** with over 12 years of experience in advanced neuroimaging and quantitative biomarkers[cite: 1]. Currently, I work as a **Research Associate** at the *Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile*[cite: 1].

My work sits at the intersection of **clinical MRI acquisition, image processing, and machine learning**[cite: 1]. I specialize in multi-field strength acquisition (ranging from low field 0.55T to high-field 3T)[cite: 1] and the development of interpretable machine learning models to identify quantitative MRI biomarkers[cite: 1] ($fMRI$, $DTI$, and $QSM$)[cite: 1]. 

Beyond scanning and data pipelines, I am committed to **Open Science**[cite: 1]. I currently serve as **Co-Director of the MRI Together Initiative**[cite: 1] and actively collaborate with international societies like the **ISMRM/ISMRT** and **ESMRMB**[cite: 1] to push for reproducible research and international education in neuroimaging[cite: 1].

### Research Interests
* **Advanced Neuroimaging:** Quantitative protocols including functional MRI ($fMRI$), Diffusion Tensor Imaging ($DTI$), and Quantitative Susceptibility Mapping ($QSM$)[cite: 1].
* **Machine Learning & Radiomics:** Developing predictive models for clinical populations, specifically focusing on Gliomas, Multiple Sclerosis, and neuropsychiatric cohorts[cite: 1].
* **Pulse Sequence Design:** Prototyping and technical visualization using the Pulseq framework[cite: 1].
* **Open Science & Reproducibility:** Code review implementation in medical journals and global educational frameworks[cite: 1].
